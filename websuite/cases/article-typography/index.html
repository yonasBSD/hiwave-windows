The Art of Typography

A comprehensive exploration of typeface design and web rendering

By Jane Designer • January 6, 2026 • 12 min read

Typography is the art and technique of arranging type to make written language legible, readable, and appealing when displayed.

The arrangement of type involves selecting typefaces, point sizes, line lengths, line-spacing, and letter-spacing, and adjusting the space between pairs of letters. The term typography is also applied to the style, arrangement, and appearance of the letters, numbers, and symbols created by the process.

Fundamentals of Type Design

Type design is a closely related craft, sometimes considered part of typography; most typographers do not design typefaces, and some type designers do not consider themselves typographers. Typography also may be used as an ornamental and decorative device, unrelated to the communication of information.

"Typography is what language looks like." — Ellen Lupton, Graphic Designer

Key Principles

  • Hierarchy: Establishing visual importance through size, weight, and position
  • Contrast: Creating visual interest through variety
  • Balance: Distributing elements evenly across the design
  • Alignment: Creating order and organization

Technical Considerations

In digital typography, we must consider font-family, font-size, line-height, and letter-spacing properties to achieve optimal readability.

body {
    font-family: Georgia, serif;
    font-size: 1.1em;
    line-height: 1.6;
    letter-spacing: 0.01em;
}

Multi-column layouts have been used in print for centuries to improve readability of long-form content. On the web, CSS columns allow us to recreate this traditional layout pattern while maintaining the flexibility of digital content.

The key to successful column layouts is finding the right balance between column width and line length. Too narrow, and the text becomes choppy; too wide, and readers lose their place between lines.
